# 4-Week Dermal Toxicity Study of BAY-3 in Rats (Study T123460-1)

## Study Design
BAY-3 was applied topically to the shaved dorsal skin of Wistar rats once daily for
28 days. Groups of 10/sex received 0, 30, or 100 mg/kg/day under semi-occlusive
dressing.

## Dermal and Clinical Observations
Dose-dependent skin irritation was observed at the application site, characterized
by erythema and mild scaling at 100 mg/kg/day. Irritation was reversible during the
recovery period. The 30 mg/kg/day group showed minimal, transient erythema only.

## Body Weight and Systemic Findings
Body weight and food consumption were unaffected. No systemic clinical signs were
observed, consistent with low dermal absorption.

## Histopathology
Microscopic examination of the application site showed mild epidermal hyperplasia
and hyperkeratosis at the high dose, consistent with local irritation. No systemic
organ findings were noted.

## Conclusion
Local skin irritation was the only test-article-related finding and was reversible.
The systemic NOAEL was 30 mg/kg/day.
