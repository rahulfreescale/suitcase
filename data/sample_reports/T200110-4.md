# Single-Dose Safety Pharmacology of BAY-7 in Dogs (Study T200110-4)

## Study Design
This cardiovascular safety pharmacology study evaluated a single intravenous dose
of BAY-7 in conscious telemetered Beagle dogs. Doses of 0, 1, and 5 mg/kg were
administered using a Latin-square crossover design (4 males).

## Cardiovascular Observations
A transient increase in heart rate was observed at the top dose (5 mg/kg),
beginning within 15 minutes of administration and resolving within approximately
2 hours. No effect on heart rate was seen at 1 mg/kg. Blood pressure and ECG
morphology, including QT interval, were unaffected at all doses.

## Clinical Observations
No abnormal clinical signs were noted. Animals remained alert and active throughout
the observation period.

## Conclusion
BAY-7 produced only a transient, dose-related heart-rate increase at 5 mg/kg with
no other cardiovascular effects. The no-effect dose for cardiovascular parameters
was 1 mg/kg.
