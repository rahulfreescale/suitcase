# 13-Week Oral Toxicity Study of BAY-1 in Rats (Study T123457-9)

## Study Design
BAY-1 was administered once daily by oral gavage to Sprague-Dawley rats for 13
weeks (91 days). Groups of 15 males and 15 females received 0, 5, 15, or
25 mg/kg/day. The study included a 4-week recovery cohort.

## Clinical Observations
No adverse clinical findings were observed at any dose level throughout the dosing
or recovery periods. Animals appeared normal at all observation timepoints.

## Body Weight and Food Consumption
Body weight gain and food consumption were comparable to controls across all dose
groups for the full duration of the study.

## Clinical Pathology
Haematology, coagulation, serum chemistry, and urinalysis parameters showed no
test-article-related changes. Values remained within historical control ranges.

## Organ Weights and Histopathology
No macroscopic or microscopic changes attributable to BAY-1 were identified. Organ
weights were unremarkable.

## Toxicokinetics
Systemic exposure (AUC and Cmax) increased approximately dose-proportionally and
showed no marked accumulation over 13 weeks.

## Conclusion
BAY-1 was well tolerated over 13 weeks of repeat oral dosing. The NOAEL was the
highest dose tested, 25 mg/kg/day, as no adverse findings were observed at any dose.
