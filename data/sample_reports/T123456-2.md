# 28-Day Oral Toxicity Study of BAY-1 in Rats (Study T123456-2)

## Study Design
BAY-1 was administered once daily by oral gavage to Sprague-Dawley rats for 28
consecutive days. Four groups of 10 males and 10 females received 0 (vehicle),
10, 25, or 50 mg/kg/day. Endpoints included clinical observations, body weight,
food consumption, clinical pathology, organ weights, and histopathology.

## Clinical Observations
During the high-dose phase (50 mg/kg/day), several animals exhibited piloerection
(hair standing on end) and ataxia (loss of coordination). A subset of high-dose
animals showed eyes partially closed during the second week of dosing. Loose
faeces was recorded intermittently in the high-dose group. No clinical signs were
observed at 10 or 25 mg/kg/day.

## Body Weight and Food Consumption
No statistically significant change in body weight was observed across groups.
Food consumption in the high-dose group was transiently reduced during week 1 but
recovered thereafter.

## Clinical Pathology
Haematology and serum chemistry parameters were within the normal range for all
groups. A slight, non-adverse increase in alanine aminotransferase (ALT) was noted
in high-dose males but was not associated with histopathological correlates.

## Organ Weights and Histopathology
No test-article-related organ weight changes or microscopic findings were observed
in any group. Liver, kidney, and brain were examined in detail.

## Conclusion
Findings were limited to mild, reversible clinical signs at the highest dose
(50 mg/kg/day). No mortality occurred during the study. The no-observed-adverse-
effect level (NOAEL) was established at 15 mg/kg/day based on the clinical signs at
the top dose.
