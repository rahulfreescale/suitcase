# 13-Week Oral Toxicity Study of BAY-3 in Dogs (Study T123465-5)

## Study Design
BAY-3 was administered orally (capsule) to Beagle dogs for 13 weeks. Groups of
4/sex received 0, 5, or 20 mg/kg/day.

## Clinical Observations
Emesis (vomiting) was observed in the high-dose group (20 mg/kg/day), often within
an hour of dosing. Decreased food consumption accompanied the emesis in high-dose
animals. No such findings occurred at 5 mg/kg/day.

## Body Weight
High-dose animals showed slightly reduced body weight gain, consistent with the
decreased food consumption.

## Clinical Pathology and Histopathology
No test-article-related haematology or serum chemistry changes were observed.
Histopathology revealed no organ findings attributable to BAY-3.

## Conclusion
Gastrointestinal effects (emesis and reduced food consumption) at the high dose were
the principal findings. The NOAEL was 5 mg/kg/day.
