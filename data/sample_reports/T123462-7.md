# 28-Day Oral Toxicity Study of BAY-9 in Mice (Study T123462-7)

## Study Design
BAY-9 was administered by oral gavage to CD-1 mice for 28 days. Groups of 10/sex
received 0, 60, or 200 mg/kg/day.

## Clinical Observations
No overt clinical signs were observed at any dose. Animals appeared normal
throughout the study.

## Clinical Pathology
High-dose animals (200 mg/kg/day) showed elevations in liver enzymes, including
alanine aminotransferase (ALT) and aspartate aminotransferase (AST), indicating a
hepatic effect.

## Organ Weights and Histopathology
Liver weights were increased at 200 mg/kg/day. Microscopic examination revealed
hepatocellular hypertrophy in high-dose animals, correlating with the enzyme
elevations and increased liver weight. No findings were seen at 60 mg/kg/day.

## Conclusion
The principal finding was hepatocellular hypertrophy with associated liver enzyme
elevations at the high dose. The NOAEL was 60 mg/kg/day.
