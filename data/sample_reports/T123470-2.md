# 28-Day Inhalation Toxicity Study of BAY-9 in Rats (Study T123470-2)

## Study Design
BAY-9 was administered to Sprague-Dawley rats by nose-only inhalation for 28 days.
Target concentrations corresponded to 0, 1, and 5 mg/kg/day equivalent doses.

## Clinical and Respiratory Observations
Respiratory tract irritation was evident at the high dose, including increased nasal
secretions and altered respiration during exposure. Low-dose animals were
unremarkable.

## Histopathology
Microscopic examination of the respiratory tract revealed nasal epithelial changes
(degeneration and inflammation of the olfactory and respiratory epithelium) at the
high dose. Lungs showed minimal changes. No findings were seen at the low dose.

## Conclusion
Local respiratory tract effects, particularly nasal epithelial changes, were the
key findings at the high dose. The NOAEL was 1 mg/kg/day equivalent.
