# Athens, Greece — Destination Guide

## Overview
Athens is the ancient cradle of democracy layered under a busy modern Mediterranean capital — the Acropolis crowning a sea of white apartment blocks, ruins tucked between cafes, and the sea never far away. It is hot, hilly, and steeped in history.

## Best Time to Visit
April to June and September to October bring warm, dry, comfortable days ideal for ruins. July and August are scorching and crowded. Winters are mild and quiet with the occasional rain.

## Neighborhoods
Plaka is the old town beneath the Acropolis — lanes, tavernas, souvenirs. Monastiraki has the flea market and street life; Syntagma is the civic centre with parliament. Koukaki and Makrigianni sit by the Acropolis Museum; Kolonaki is upscale and chic.

## Things to Do
Climb the Acropolis and see the Parthenon, tour the superb Acropolis Museum, wander the Ancient Agora and Plaka, watch the guard change at Syntagma, and take in the sunset from Lycabettus or Filopappou Hill. Day-trip to Cape Sounion or the islands.

## Getting Around
The Metro is modern, largely step-free and connects the airport, centre and port; most stations have lifts. Buses and trams are mostly accessible. But the historic centre is hilly with marble paving, cobbles and stepped lanes that get slick — walking is the main way around Plaka and the ruins.

## Budget Notes
One of Europe's better-value capitals. Site entry is modest and a combined ticket covers the Acropolis and several ruins; many viewpoints are free. Tavernas and souvlaki are cheap; the Acropolis Museum and taxis are reasonable.

## Family & Accessibility
Athens is challenging for wheelchair users because its heart is ancient and hilly, but it has improved. The Acropolis now has a wheelchair lift and an accessible path to the top (though the marble is uneven and the lift can be out of service — verify first), and the Acropolis Museum is fully accessible and excellent. The Metro is step-free and a real asset. But Plaka's lanes are cobbled, stepped and steep, and marble paving citywide is slippery and uneven. Flat modern avenues and the museum make parts easy; the ruins remain hard.

## Popular but Challenging
- Acropolis & Parthenon — A wheelchair lift and an accessible path reach the summit, but the ground is uneven ancient marble and rock, often slippery, and the lift is periodically out of service.
- Ancient Agora — An open archaeological site with gravel paths, uneven ground and slopes; the reconstructed Stoa has some access but much of it is rough underfoot.
- Plaka old town — Charming but built on the Acropolis slope with cobbles, marble, steps and steep lanes; some flatter shopping streets exist but much is hard to wheel or push a stroller through.
- Monastiraki & flea market — A lively flat-ish square but the market lanes are crowded, uneven and tight; manageable in parts but a squeeze with wheels or a stroller.
- Lycabettus Hill — A funicular reaches near the summit for panoramic views, easing the climb, but the very top and some paths still involve steps.
