# Toronto, Canada — Destination Guide

## Overview
Toronto is Canada's biggest city — a clean, safe, hyper-diverse metropolis on Lake Ontario, where a modern downtown of glass towers meets 150+ cultures in its neighborhoods. Mostly flat and well-organized, it's an easy city to navigate.

## Best Time to Visit
Late spring through early fall (May–October) is the sweet spot — warm, green, and packed with festivals and patios. Summer is warm and humid; winter is long, cold and snowy but the underground PATH keeps you moving.

## Neighborhoods
Downtown holds the CN Tower, financial core and waterfront. Kensington Market and Chinatown are eclectic and food-rich; the Distillery District is cobbled Victorian charm; Queen West is arts and design; Yorkville is upscale; the Beaches and the Islands offer waterfront escape.

## Things to Do
Go up the CN Tower, explore the Royal Ontario Museum and the AGO, wander Kensington Market and St. Lawrence Market, ferry to the Toronto Islands for skyline views, and browse the Distillery District. Catch a Raptors or Leafs game and eat around the world.

## Getting Around
Toronto is flat and gridded. The subway is partly accessible — many stations have elevators but not all, so check; the newer streetcars and all buses are fully accessible with ramps. Downtown and the waterfront are flat and smooth; the underground PATH network links downtown step-free (with elevators) in winter.

## Budget Notes
Mid-range. The waterfront, Kensington Market, and neighborhood wandering are free; the AGO has free evening windows. The CN Tower, ROM and sports tickets are the bigger costs; the city's incredible cheap-eats scene keeps food affordable.

## Family & Accessibility
Toronto is flat, modern and broadly accessible: downtown, the waterfront, the ferry terminal and the major attractions (CN Tower with lifts, ROM, AGO) are step-free, and the streetcar and bus fleets are fully accessible. The subway's older stations are the gap — not all have elevators — so plan rail trips. The cobbled Distillery District is the main uneven surface. The winter PATH is fully step-free via elevators. Very manageable for wheels, strollers and seniors.

## Popular but Challenging
- Distillery District — A pedestrian Victorian district paved in historic cobblestones that are charming but jarring and uneven for wheels and strollers; shops and cafes are mostly step-free once inside.
