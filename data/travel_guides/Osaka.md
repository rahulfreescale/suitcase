# Osaka, Japan — Destination Guide

## Overview
Osaka is Japan's brash, food-obsessed second city — neon-lit canals, a castle, warm and direct locals, and street food that defines the place. Flat, modern and superbly organized, it's one of the easier big Japanese cities to get around.

## Best Time to Visit
Spring (late March–April) for cherry blossoms and autumn (October–November) for foliage are the loveliest and busiest. Summer is hot and humid; winter is cool and mild. Shoulder seasons are ideal.

## Neighborhoods
Namba and Dotonbori are the neon nightlife-and-food heart; Umeda (Kita) is the northern hub of skyscrapers and stations; Shinsekai is retro and gritty; Osaka Castle sits in its park to the east; Nakanoshima is the museum-lined river island.

## Things to Do
Eat your way through Dotonbori and Kuromon Market (takoyaki, okonomiyaki), visit Osaka Castle, ride to the Umeda Sky Building's floating garden, day-trip to nearby Kyoto or Nara, and enjoy Universal Studios Japan. Osaka is above all about food.

## Getting Around
Osaka's subway and rail network is extensive, clean and largely accessible with lifts, tactile paving and level boarding at most stations; staff readily assist. The city is flat, and buses are accessible too. Dotonbori and the arcades are flat and walkable, if crowded. Overall very manageable.

## Budget Notes
Good value by big-city standards, and cheaper than Tokyo. Street food is famously affordable, the castle park and river walks are free/cheap, and many sights are low-cost. Universal Studios, the Sky Building, and dining out are the bigger spends.

## Family & Accessibility
Osaka is one of Japan's more accessible cities: flat terrain, a subway network with widespread lifts and helpful staff, and modern attractions with step-free access. Osaka Castle has lifts inside (though the surrounding park has some slopes and the outer stone approaches are uneven), the covered arcades and Dotonbori are flat, and the museums and Sky Building are accessible. Crowds in the food districts are the main friction. Strollers and wheelchairs do well across most of the city.
