# Jaipur, India — Destination Guide

## Overview
Jaipur, the 'Pink City', is the romantic capital of Rajasthan — a walled old city of rose-hued facades, grand palaces, hilltop forts, and vivid bazaars. Rich and photogenic, it's also historic, crowded, and built for another era of mobility.

## Best Time to Visit
October to March is the pleasant season — sunny, dry and cool. Summers (April–June) are punishingly hot; the monsoon brings some relief and greenery. Winter is by far the best time to visit.

## Neighborhoods
The walled Pink City holds the City Palace, Hawa Mahal, Jantar Mantar and the bazaars. Amber (Amer) to the north has the great fort; the newer areas of C-Scheme and Civil Lines are leafier and modern; Nahargarh and Jaigarh forts crown the surrounding hills.

## Things to Do
Tour the City Palace and the astronomical Jantar Mantar, photograph the Hawa Mahal, ride up to the magnificent Amber Fort, shop the bazaars for textiles and gems, and watch sunset from Nahargarh. Eat Rajasthani thali and dal baati churma.

## Getting Around
Jaipur is spread out and best covered by app cabs, autos or a hired car. The walled old city is flat but intensely crowded, with uneven pavements, encroached footpaths and traffic; the forts are on steep hills. There is a small accessible metro line but limited coverage. Walking the bazaars is possible but chaotic.

## Budget Notes
Very good value. Palace and fort entries are modest, bazaars and street food are cheap, and heritage stays can be affordable. A hired car with driver for the day is inexpensive and the easiest way to cover the forts. Luxury palace hotels are the splurge.

## Family & Accessibility
Jaipur is difficult for wheelchair users: the historic monuments were built with steps, ramps to upper levels are rare, the old city's pavements are broken and crowded, and the hilltop forts involve steep climbs. The City Palace and some museums have partial ground-floor access, and modern hotels and the newer areas are easier. Amber Fort has a steep approach (jeeps help reach the top, but interiors are stepped). A hired car eases transport, but the sights themselves are largely stair-based. Plan for significant assistance.

## Popular but Challenging
- Amber (Amer) Fort — A magnificent hilltop fort reached by a steep ramp/road (jeeps available) with cobbled ascents; inside, the levels are connected by stairs and steep ramps with no accessible route through.
- Nahargarh Fort — A hilltop fort with panoramic sunset views reached by car up a winding road, but the fort interior and ramparts are stepped and uneven; the approach terrace offers views.
- Johari & Bapu Bazaar — The vibrant bazaars are flat but extremely crowded with broken, encroached footpaths and traffic; navigable slowly with help but a real challenge for wheels and strollers.
- Jaigarh Fort — A rugged hill fort reached by a steep road, with stepped, uneven interiors and the giant cannon; not accessible beyond the entrance area.
