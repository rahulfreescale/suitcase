# Chicago, USA — Destination Guide

## Overview
Chicago is the grand city of the American Midwest — a flat grid of bold architecture on Lake Michigan, with a spectacular skyline, lakefront parks, world-class museums, and deep neighborhoods. Its flatness and planning make it unusually easy to get around.

## Best Time to Visit
Late spring (May–June) and early fall (September–October) are ideal — warm, clear, festival-filled. Summer is lively but can be humid; winter is genuinely brutal with lake-effect cold and wind.

## Neighborhoods
The Loop is the downtown core of architecture and theatres; the Magnificent Mile is upscale shopping; River North has galleries and dining. Wicker Park and Logan Square are hip; Pilsen is Mexican-American culture and murals; Hyde Park holds the university and museums.

## Things to Do
Admire the skyline on an architecture river cruise, visit Millennium Park and 'The Bean', see the Art Institute, go up Willis Tower or 360 Chicago, walk the lakefront, and explore neighborhood food from deep-dish to taquerias. Catch blues, comedy, or a ballgame.

## Getting Around
Chicago is flat and gridded, making it walkable and easy to navigate. The 'L' train is iconic but many elevated stations are stairs-only — check accessible-station listings; buses are 100% accessible with ramps and kneeling. The lakefront and Loop are flat and smooth; the accessible bus network fills gaps well.

## Budget Notes
Mid-range. Millennium Park, the lakefront, and 'The Bean' are free; the Art Institute has free days for Illinois residents. Deep-dish and neighborhood eats are good value; the observation decks, cruises and downtown hotels are the bigger costs.

## Family & Accessibility
Chicago is one of the more accessible major US cities: dead flat, well-planned, with wide smooth sidewalks and a fully accessible bus fleet. Millennium Park, the Riverwalk, the lakefront trail, and the big museums (Art Institute, Field, Shedd) are all step-free with lifts. The main gap is the older 'L' stations, many of which lack elevators, so plan rail routes or use buses. Winter ice is the seasonal hazard. Broadly excellent for wheels, strollers and seniors.
