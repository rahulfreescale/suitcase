# Madrid, Spain — Destination Guide

## Overview
Madrid is Spain's high, sunny capital — a city of grand plazas, golden-age art, sprawling parks, and a late-night rhythm all its own. Its elegant centre is compact and walkable, if hillier than it first appears.

## Best Time to Visit
Spring (April–June) and autumn (September–October) are ideal — warm, clear, and comfortable. July and August are fiercely hot and many locals leave. Winters are cold, crisp and quiet.

## Neighborhoods
The historic centre runs from Puerta del Sol to the Royal Palace and Plaza Mayor. The Prado and its sister museums line the Paseo del Prado; Retiro Park sits beside them. Malasaña and Chueca are hip and nightlife-driven; La Latina is tapas and Sunday markets; Salamanca is upscale shopping.

## Things to Do
Tour the Prado, Reina Sofía (Guernica), and Thyssen museums, wander Retiro Park and its glass palace, see the Royal Palace, soak up Plaza Mayor and Puerta del Sol, and graze tapas in La Latina. Watch the sunset from the Temple of Debod.

## Getting Around
Madrid's Metro is one of the world's largest and increasingly step-free, with lifts at most central and newer stations (check the accessible-station map). Buses are 100% low-floor and ramped. The centre is walkable but has gentle hills and some cobbled plazas.

## Budget Notes
Mid-range and often better value than Paris or London. The big museums have free-entry evening windows; parks and plazas are free. Tapas and menú del día lunches are excellent value; the Royal Palace and rooftop bars cost more.

## Family & Accessibility
Madrid is fairly accessible: the centre is walkable with mostly smooth wide pavements, the museums (Prado, Reina Sofía, Thyssen) are all lift-served with free disabled entry, and Retiro Park is broad and largely flat. The Metro is partly step-free — many but not all stations have lifts, so plan routes. Some old-town plazas (Plaza Mayor, La Latina) have cobbles and gentle slopes. Buses are a reliable step-free fallback. Overall manageable for wheels, prams and seniors with a little planning.

## Popular but Challenging
- Mercado de San Miguel — A historic covered market at street level but extremely crowded and tight between stalls; wheeling or a stroller is difficult at busy times.
