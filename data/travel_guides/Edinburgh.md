# Edinburgh, United Kingdom — Destination Guide

## Overview
Edinburgh is Scotland's dramatic capital — a medieval Old Town of dark closes climbing to a castle on a crag, beside an elegant Georgian New Town, all under big northern skies. Compact and walkable, it is also famously steep and stepped.

## Best Time to Visit
May, June and September offer the mildest, driest weather and long daylight. August is festival month — electric but packed and pricey. Winters are dark, cold and atmospheric with Hogmanay.

## Neighborhoods
The Old Town runs down the Royal Mile from the Castle to Holyrood, all closes and wynds. The New Town below is Georgian grandeur, shops and galleries. Stockbridge is a charming village-y quarter; Leith is the revitalised waterfront; Bruntsfield and Marchmont are leafy and local.

## Things to Do
Explore Edinburgh Castle and walk the Royal Mile to Holyrood Palace, climb Arthur's Seat or Calton Hill for views, wander the National Museum and galleries, and browse the New Town. Time a visit for the summer festivals if you can handle crowds.

## Getting Around
The centre is compact and walkable but genuinely steep, with hills, staircases (closes) and cobbled setts throughout the Old Town. Buses and the tram are modern, low-floor and accessible and the best option on the hills. The New Town is flatter and easier; the Old Town's cobbles and gradients are the main challenge.

## Budget Notes
Mid-range. The superb National Museum of Scotland and the national galleries are free; the castle, Holyrood and the festivals cost more. Walking the city and the hills is free. August prices spike hard for accommodation.

## Family & Accessibility
Edinburgh is scenic but hard for wheelchair users in the Old Town, which is steep, cobbled and threaded with stepped closes; Arthur's Seat and the crags are for the able-bodied. But the flat Georgian New Town is much easier, the buses and tram are fully accessible, and the big draws — the free National Museum, the galleries, and even Edinburgh Castle (which has a mobility vehicle and accessible route to the upper areas) — accommodate wheels with planning. Strollers manage the New Town and flat paths; the Old Town's setts are jarring.

## Popular but Challenging
- Edinburgh Castle — A courtesy mobility vehicle carries wheelchair users up the steep cobbled entry, and much of the upper castle is then accessible, but some historic buildings have stairs and the setts are rough.
- Royal Mile — The famous street slopes steeply from castle to palace over cobbled setts, with stepped closes off each side; wheelable downhill with effort but jarring and tiring, hard uphill.
- Arthur's Seat — An extinct volcano reached only by steep, rough, unpaved hill paths; no accessible route to the summit.
- Calton Hill — Panoramic hill with monuments; there is a longer accessible path from the eastern road side avoiding the main staircase, but the direct route is stepped.
