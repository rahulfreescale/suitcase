# Singapore — Destination Guide

## Overview
Singapore is a gleaming, hyper-organized city-state where futuristic gardens,
colonial heritage, and a dazzling food scene meet at the crossroads of Southeast
Asia. Clean, safe, hot, and remarkably efficient, it's one of the world's easiest
big cities to visit.

## Best Time to Visit
Singapore is hot and humid year-round (just north of the equator). February–April is
slightly drier. The wettest months are November–January. There's no bad time — just
plan for heat, afternoon downpours, and air-conditioned breaks.

## Neighborhoods
Marina Bay is the modern showpiece (Gardens by the Bay, the iconic Marina Bay Sands).
Chinatown, Little India, and Kampong Glam are the vibrant heritage quarters. Orchard
Road is the shopping spine. Sentosa is the resort island of beaches and attractions
just offshore.

## Things to Do
Explore Gardens by the Bay and the Supertree Grove, ride to the top of Marina Bay
Sands, wander the hawker centres for cheap Michelin-level food, visit the world-class
Singapore Zoo and Night Safari, and take the kids to Sentosa. The heritage districts
reward slow wandering.

## Getting Around
Singapore has one of the world's best public transport systems: the MRT is clean,
cheap, air-conditioned, and famously accessible, backed by buses and affordable taxis.
The city is walkable in stretches but the heat makes transit and covered walkways the
smart choice.

## Budget Notes
Singapore can be pricey for lodging and alcohol, but eating is a bargain — hawker
centres serve outstanding meals for a few dollars. Many gardens, heritage streets, and
the light shows at Marina Bay and the Supertrees are free. Transit is inexpensive.

## Family & Accessibility
Singapore is exceptionally accessible and family-friendly — flat, modern, and built
with universal design in mind. The MRT is one of the world's most wheelchair- and
stroller-accessible metros (lifts, level boarding, wide gates throughout), and buses
are low-floor. Gardens by the Bay is famously easy — wide, flat, paved paths, ramps,
and even the elevated Supertree walkway and cooled conservatories are accessible.
Sentosa has accessible attractions and beach tracks; Marina Bay's promenades are broad
and level. Attractions widely offer wheelchair/stroller rental, nursing rooms, and
baby-changing facilities, and the universal heat means most everything is
air-conditioned — a relief with a toddler. Toddlers do well here: the Zoo, the
S.E.A. Aquarium, and Gardens by the Bay are calm-paced and stroller-easy. The main
caveats are minor: the heat and humidity mean frequent breaks and hydration, some older
heritage-shophouse streets have narrow or uneven five-foot-ways, and a few hawker
centres get very crowded. Overall, one of the smoothest cities anywhere for wheels of
any kind.

## Popular but Challenging
- Older shophouse five-foot-ways (Chinatown, Little India) — charming but narrow, stepped, and uneven in places; a stroller sometimes has to detour to the main pavement.
- Peak-hour hawker centres — the food is the whole point, but the busiest ones (e.g. Maxwell, Lau Pa Sat at lunch) get elbow-to-elbow and hard to navigate with a stroller. Alt: go early or off-peak.
- Midday outdoor sightseeing — not a place but a hazard: the equatorial heat is punishing for toddlers and wheelchair users by noon. Alt: outdoor sights early/late, indoor/air-conditioned in the middle of the day.
- Pulau Ubin / rustic nature spots — a lovely throwback island, but unpaved, rustic, and not wheelchair- or stroller-friendly. Alt: the accessible boardwalks at Sungei Buloh or Gardens by the Bay.
