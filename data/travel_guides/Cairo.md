# Cairo, Egypt — Destination Guide

## Overview
Cairo is the sprawling, chaotic, endlessly fascinating capital of Egypt — a megacity of 20+ million where the Pyramids meet medieval Islamic quarters, Coptic churches, and the Nile. Ancient wonders sit beside relentless modern bustle, dust, and traffic.

## Best Time to Visit
October to April is the comfortable season — warm days, cool nights. Summer (May–September) is brutally hot, especially at the exposed Pyramids. Winter is the ideal window for sightseeing.

## Neighborhoods
Downtown (Wust el-Balad) has faded colonial grandeur and Tahrir Square; Islamic Cairo holds the medieval mosques and the Khan el-Khalili bazaar; Coptic (Old) Cairo has ancient churches; Zamalek is the leafy island district; Giza to the west is the Pyramids and the new Grand Egyptian Museum.

## Things to Do
Stand before the Pyramids of Giza and the Sphinx, explore the treasures at the Grand Egyptian Museum, wander Islamic Cairo's mosques and the Khan el-Khalili bazaar, visit Coptic Cairo, and cruise or dine along the Nile. Day-trip to Saqqara and Memphis.

## Getting Around
Cairo is flat but famously chaotic, with heavy traffic, dust, and uneven, obstructed footpaths; crossing streets is daunting. The metro is cheap and has some accessible newer stations but is packed; app cabs (Uber/Careem) are the practical way for visitors to get around. The Pyramids plateau is sandy and uneven; the modern museums are accessible.

## Budget Notes
Very cheap for visitors. Local food, markets, mosques and churches cost little; the Pyramids and museum entries are modest. A private driver/guide for the day is affordable and eases the chaos. Nile dinner cruises and upscale hotels are the bigger costs.

## Family & Accessibility
Cairo is challenging: footpaths are uneven and obstructed, traffic is overwhelming, and many historic mosques and the sandy Pyramids plateau are hard for wheels. But there's good news — the new Grand Egyptian Museum is modern and fully accessible with ramps and lifts, the Pyramids can be viewed and approached by car/accessible vehicle on the plateau (even if the sand and the tomb interiors are not accessible), and app cabs handle transport. Islamic and Coptic Cairo's old lanes and stepped monuments are the hardest. Plan around the accessible museum and driven plateau visits.

## Popular but Challenging
- Pyramids of Giza & Sphinx — The plateau can be toured by car/accessible vehicle to viewpoints of the pyramids and Sphinx, but the ground is sand and uneven rock and entering the pyramids means steep, cramped stair passages with no access.
- Khan el-Khalili Bazaar — The medieval market is flat but a dense warren of narrow, uneven, crowded lanes with thresholds; navigable slowly with help but very hard for wheels and strollers.
- Saqqara & Step Pyramid — The desert necropolis can be approached by vehicle to viewpoints, but the sandy, uneven site and tomb interiors are not accessible.
