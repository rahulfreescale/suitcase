# Melbourne, Australia — Destination Guide

## Overview
Melbourne is Australia's cultured, coffee-obsessed second city — a flat, gridded metropolis of laneway art, grand Victorian arcades, sport, and a legendary food-and-arts scene on the Yarra River. Orderly and largely flat, it's one of the most accessible big cities anywhere.

## Best Time to Visit
Autumn (March–May) and spring (September–November) are the loveliest — mild and pleasant, though Melbourne is famous for 'four seasons in one day'. Summer is warm and event-packed; winter is cool and grey. Pack layers year-round.

## Neighborhoods
The CBD grid holds the laneways, arcades and Federation Square. Fitzroy and Collingwood are hip and street-arty; St Kilda is the bayside beach-and-pier suburb; Southbank and the Arts precinct line the river; Carlton is Italian-flavored near the university and gardens; Richmond and Brunswick are food-diverse.

## Things to Do
Explore the CBD laneways and street art, browse the Queen Victoria Market and Victorian arcades, visit the NGV and Melbourne Museum, relax in the Royal Botanic Gardens, ride the free City Circle tram, and head to St Kilda for the beach and penguins. Catch cricket or footy at the MCG.

## Getting Around
Melbourne is flat and grid-planned, making it very walkable. The tram network is the world's largest, and the newer low-floor trams are accessible (older ones aren't, and many stops need level platforms) — check accessible routes; the free City Circle tram loops the CBD. Trains and buses are largely accessible. Wide smooth footpaths make the CBD, riverside and gardens excellent for wheels and strollers.

## Budget Notes
Mid-to-expensive (Australia is pricey). The laneways, markets, gardens, riverside and street art are free; the NGV's main collection is free. Coffee and multicultural eats span budgets; sport tickets, nicer dining and hotels are the bigger costs.

## Family & Accessibility
Melbourne is one of the world's more accessible cities: flat, gridded, with wide smooth footpaths, and a strong emphasis on access. The CBD, Federation Square, Southbank riverside and the Royal Botanic Gardens are excellent for wheelchairs and strollers; the major museums (NGV, Melbourne Museum) are fully accessible; and the MCG and modern venues have great facilities. The main caveat is the tram system — newer low-floor trams and level 'superstops' are accessible but older trams and some stops are not, so plan routes. Overall, superb access.
