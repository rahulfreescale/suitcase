# Berlin, Germany — Destination Guide

## Overview
Berlin is a vast, flat, low-rise capital defined by 20th-century history and 21st-century reinvention — Cold War scars, world-class museums, sprawling parks, and a famously unpolished creative energy. Space and flatness make it unusually navigable.

## Best Time to Visit
May to September is warm and green with long days and open-air everything. Spring and early autumn are pleasant and quieter. Winters are cold and grey but the museums and cafes shine.

## Neighborhoods
Mitte holds the historic core — Museum Island, the Brandenburg Gate, government quarter. Kreuzberg and Neukölln are multicultural and nightlife-heavy; Prenzlauer Berg is leafy and family-friendly; Charlottenburg in the west is grander and calmer around its palace.

## Things to Do
Stand at the Brandenburg Gate, walk the East Side Gallery stretch of the Wall, tour Museum Island, visit the glass-domed Reichstag, reflect at the Holocaust Memorial, and relax in the huge Tiergarten. Explore Kreuzberg's food and the city's endless galleries.

## Getting Around
Berlin's flat terrain and mostly step-free U-Bahn/S-Bahn make it one of Europe's more accessible cities; most stations have lifts and trains are level-boarding. Buses and trams are fully accessible. Wide, smooth pavements make walking and wheeling easy. Distances are large, so transit helps.

## Budget Notes
Relatively affordable for a Western capital. Many memorials and the Reichstag are free; a Museum Island pass bundles the big collections. Parks, the Wall, and street food (currywurst, döner) are cheap. Overall good value versus Paris or London.

## Family & Accessibility
Berlin is among the most wheelchair- and stroller-friendly major cities in Europe: flat, spacious, with wide smooth pavements and a largely step-free transit network (check lift status as some are out at times). The big museums, the Reichstag dome (via lift, free, pre-book), the Holocaust Memorial, and the Tiergarten are all accessible. Cobbles appear in a few older squares and around Museum Island, but broadly the city is easy for wheels, prams and seniors.

## Popular but Challenging
- Memorial to the Murdered Jews of Europe — The field of stelae has an undulating stone-paved ground that is step-free but gently uneven; the underground information centre is fully accessible by lift.
