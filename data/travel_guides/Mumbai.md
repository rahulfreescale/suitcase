# Mumbai, India — Destination Guide

## Overview
Mumbai is India's dense, dynamic financial and film capital — a peninsula of colonial-era grandeur, sprawling markets, seaside promenades, and relentless energy, home to over 20 million people. It's flat along the coast but crowded, chaotic, and uneven underfoot.

## Best Time to Visit
November to February is the best — dry, warm and pleasant. The monsoon (June–September) brings dramatic, flooding rains; the pre-monsoon months are hot and humid. Winter is the clear window.

## Neighborhoods
Colaba and Fort hold the colonial architecture, the Gateway and museums. Marine Drive sweeps along the bay; Bandra is the trendy suburban heart of cafes and Bollywood; Dharavi is the famous informal settlement; the Bandra-Kurla Complex is modern business.

## Things to Do
See the Gateway of India and take the ferry to the Elephanta Caves, stroll Marine Drive at sunset, visit the CSMVS museum and the Prince of Wales area, admire the UNESCO Victorian and Art Deco ensembles, and eat street food from vada pav to Mohammed Ali Road. Catch the buzz of a Bollywood city.

## Getting Around
Mumbai is flat along the coast but intensely crowded, and its footpaths are uneven, broken, and often obstructed. The suburban trains are the city's lifeline but are not wheelchair-accessible and are extremely packed; the newer metro lines are accessible with lifts. Taxis, autos and app cabs are the practical way for visitors. Walking the seafronts is easy; the markets and older lanes are hard.

## Budget Notes
Wide range but generally cheap for visitors. Street food, local sights, the promenades and markets cost very little; the Gateway, museums and Elephanta ferry are modest. Luxury hotels (the Taj), fine dining and Bandra cafes are where costs climb.

## Family & Accessibility
Mumbai is challenging for wheelchair users: pavements are uneven, broken and crowded, the lifeline suburban trains are inaccessible and packed, and many older buildings and markets have steps and no ramps. That said, the flat seaside promenades (Marine Drive, Bandstand) are wheelable, the newer metro and some modern museums and malls are accessible, and the major museum (CSMVS) has improved access. App cabs handle transport. Elephanta involves a boat and a steep climb. Broadly, plan around the flat waterfronts and accessible pockets.

## Popular but Challenging
- Elephanta Caves — Reached by a ferry then a long steep staircase (or a small toy train and more steps) up to rock-cut cave temples; no accessible route to the caves.
- Haji Ali Dargah — The mosque-tomb sits on an islet reached by a long narrow causeway that floods at high tide and is crowded and uneven; ramps are being improved but access is hard and tidal.
- Crawford Market / Colaba Causeway — Bustling markets that are flat but crowded, with uneven floors, thresholds and tight lanes; navigable slowly but a real squeeze for wheels and strollers.
