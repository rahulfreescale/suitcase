# Los Angeles, USA — Destination Guide

## Overview
Los Angeles is a sprawling, sun-soaked megacity of distinct neighborhoods spread across a coastal basin — Hollywood glamour, beach towns, world-class museums, and canyons, all stitched together by freeways. It is horizontal, car-shaped, and mostly flat where it counts.

## Best Time to Visit
Spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) bring warm, clear, comfortable days. Summers are hot inland but mild at the coast; winters are mild with occasional rain. It's a year-round destination.

## Neighborhoods
Hollywood and West Hollywood are entertainment and nightlife; Santa Monica and Venice are beach and boardwalk; Downtown has museums and food halls; Beverly Hills and Bel-Air are wealth; Silver Lake and Los Feliz are hip and hilly; Pasadena sits inland with gardens.

## Things to Do
Walk the Hollywood Walk of Fame, ride to the Griffith Observatory for the skyline and Hollywood Sign, stroll Santa Monica Pier and Venice Boardwalk, visit the Getty Center and LACMA, and explore Downtown's food halls. Beaches, hikes, and theme parks ring the city.

## Getting Around
LA is built for cars and is enormous; a rental is the practical way around. The expanding Metro rail and buses are modern and fully accessible (level boarding, ramps) but coverage is patchy. The flat basin neighborhoods (Santa Monica, Downtown) are walkable and accessible; the hills (Silver Lake, canyons) are steep.

## Budget Notes
Mid-to-expensive, with wide range. Beaches, the Walk of Fame, Griffith Observatory and the Getty are free (parking aside). Theme parks, dining and hotels can be pricey; food trucks and diverse neighborhood eats are great value.

## Family & Accessibility
LA is largely flat and modern where visitors spend time, making much of it wheelchair- and stroller-friendly: the Getty (with trams and lifts), LACMA, Santa Monica's beachfront path, and Downtown are all accessible, and Metro rail/buses are fully step-free. The beaches offer accessible mats and beach wheelchairs at several piers. Challenges are the car-dependence, long distances, and the hillside neighborhoods and canyon hikes, which are steep. Overall one of the easier big US cities for access if you drive.

## Popular but Challenging
- Griffith Park hikes / Hollywood Sign trail — The trails to viewpoints and the sign are steep, unpaved fire roads with no accessible route; not suitable for wheelchairs, strollers or limited mobility.
