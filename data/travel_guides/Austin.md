# Austin, USA — Destination Guide

## Overview
Austin is Texas's laid-back capital — a green, growing city of live music, food trucks, lakes and tech, wrapped in a 'keep it weird' spirit. Warm and mostly flat near the river, it's spread out and car-oriented but easy where visitors gather.

## Best Time to Visit
Spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) are ideal — warm, green, and festival-rich (SXSW, ACL). Summers are long and very hot; winters are mild. Aim for the shoulder seasons.

## Neighborhoods
Downtown holds the music venues, Sixth Street and the Capitol; South Congress (SoCo) is boutiques and food trucks; East Austin is the hip, artsy, food-driven side; Zilker and Barton Springs are the green heart; the Domain up north is upscale shopping.

## Things to Do
Hear live music on South Congress and Red River, swim at Barton Springs, watch the bats emerge from Congress Avenue Bridge, tour the State Capitol, kayak Lady Bird Lake, and eat barbecue and breakfast tacos. Time it for SXSW or ACL if you dare the crowds.

## Getting Around
Austin is spread out and car-oriented; a rental or rideshare is the practical choice. Buses are accessible but coverage is limited. Downtown, the Capitol grounds and the Lady Bird Lake trail are flat and walkable; the terrain gently rolls in places. Distances between districts are real.

## Budget Notes
Mid-range. Live music (many free shows), the bat bridge, Barton Springs (low fee), the Capitol and the lake trail are cheap or free. Barbecue, food trucks and tacos are great value; festivals, downtown hotels and nightlife are the bigger costs.

## Family & Accessibility
Austin is mostly flat where visitors go and fairly accessible: the State Capitol is fully accessible, the Lady Bird Lake hike-and-bike trail is largely flat and paved (with accessible sections), and downtown and South Congress have step-free sidewalks. Barton Springs has an accessible entry lift. The gaps are the car-dependence, long distances, limited transit, and some gravel/natural areas. Warm weather aside, it's a manageable city for wheels and strollers around the core.

## Popular but Challenging
- Texas Hill Country / Mount Bonnell — The classic Mount Bonnell viewpoint is reached by a steep staircase with no accessible route; the wider Hill Country is rugged.
